PHASE / 02 2019 — 2023 Sports Science
HOW I LEARNED TO QUESTION

Training wasn't enough anymore.

Where instinct met instrumentation, and the body became a system worth measuring.

I wanted to know why some improve, and others don't.

So I broke performance down.

Force. Time. Recovery. Adaptation.

Most people guess. I stopped guessing.

Subject · Hover any node 5 vectors measured · not assumed
VECTOR · 01 / FORCE

What you can produce.

Output. Not effort. Effort is the input. Force is what actually leaves the body and moves the world. Most training programmes confuse the two.

output = (strength × technique × intent)
VECTOR · 02 / TIME

How fast you can apply it.

Force without time is theatre. The window between intent and contact decides whether the work shows up on the day. This is where most athletes plateau.

power = force / time-to-contact
VECTOR · 03 / RECOVERY

How fast you return to baseline.

Training breaks you. Recovery is what compounds. The athletes who improve aren't the ones who train hardest. They're the ones who recover faster between sessions.

capacity = stress × recovery rate
VECTOR · 04 / ADAPTATION

How the system updates itself.

The body doesn't get better from training. It gets better from adapting to training. Same input, different responders. Find the variable that's actually moving and stop celebrating volume.

progress = stimulus × responder profile
VECTOR · 05 / SIGNAL

What you actually measure.

Most metrics are vanity. The useful ones are leading: HRV before fatigue. Bar speed before failure. The signal arrives before the result, if you're listening for it.

decision = data − noise
